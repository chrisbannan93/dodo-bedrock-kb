# Dodo Copy Formatting Rules

## Salutation
- Use: **Hey <FIRSTNAME>** (no comma).
- Avoid formal “regarding” lines after salutations.

## Signoff
- **Email:**
  - Thanks
  - The Dodo team
- **SMS (if space allows):** The Dodo team
- **Direct mail:**
  - Thanks
  - The Dodo team

## Punctuation (keep it minimal)
- No comma after the salutation.
- No full stops after dot points.
- No full stop after the signoff.
- No full stops in subheads.
- No full stops at the end of subject lines.
- Never use semicolons.
- Avoid exclamation marks where possible (max one per communication).

## Contractions (use where possible)
- Use you’re (not you are)
- Use you’ll (not you will)
- Use wouldn’t (not would not)

## Subject lines
- Can include first name personalisation.
- Emojis are acceptable.
- Focus on the primary message.
- No full stops.

## Preheaders
- **Service comms:** Always “What you need to know”.
- **Marketing comms:** Flexible based on the message.

## Layout and alignment
- Header banner copy can be centred.
- All other copy (including subheads) is left aligned.

## Fonts and sizes
- **Email H1 banner:** Arial 32px bold (Gilroy if it’s an image banner).
- **Body copy:** Arial 15px.
- **Subheads:** Arial 15px, bold, purple #470A68.
- **Direct mail:** Gilroy Bold 15px (same subhead treatment as email).

## Link formatting
- Weblinks are bold, not underlined.
- Display without https:// and without www.
- Link colour: purple #470A68.
- Match body copy font size (15px).

## Phone number formatting
- Phone numbers should be **bold**, **hyperlinked**, and **purple #470A68**.
- Match body copy font size (15px).

## Labels
- Display labels in leading caps, e.g., **Account Number:**
- Internet labels: **Account Number:**
- Mobile labels: **Service Number:**
- Energy labels: **Account Number:** / **Electricity Account Number:** / **Gas Account Number:**

## Dates, times, numbers
- Dates: long-form Australian format, e.g., **1 January 2025** (year optional).
- Times: include am/pm and time zone, e.g., **8am–7pm AEST**.
- Numbers: spell out ten or less; use numerals for 11+.

## Terminology usage
- Use **Log in** when directing customers to log in to My Dodo.
- Use **login** when referring to entering login details.
